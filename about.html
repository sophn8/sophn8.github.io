About
A native New Yorker, I recently graduated from Columbia University's School of International and Public Affairs where I studied Data Analytics, Management, and Urban Policy. I earned my BA in Political Science and Interdisciplinary American Studies from UC Berkeley, and a certificate in Designing for Social Innovation and Leadership from the UN Centre for Peace.

I have professional experience in data analysis, program management, and product research, along with building internal tools to monitor platform abuse. A former Board Member of the New York City chapter of the National Asian Pacific American Women’s Forum and Participatory Review Committee Member at the New York Women’s Foundation’s Fund for Girls and Young Women of Color, I'm also passionate about equity, opportunity, and inclusion. My most thrilling life experience to date was when I served as a Fulbright grantee in Malaysia: I taught English classes, managed peer mentorship and social entrepreneurship programs, facilitated skills-based technology workshops, and implemented public diplomacy initiatives in the town of Gopeng.

I'm adroit at making things happen, and pride myself with my strong work ethic and organizational skills. My peers in Malaysia nominated me for these superlatives, and I think they most uniquely capture my personality and attitude:
  • Most likely to create order out of chaos
  • Most likely to manage a Form 4 Malaysian boy band
  • Most likely to be the next Bill Gates
  • Most likely to rise like a phoenix from a shitty thing
  • Best at naming things
