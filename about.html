About
Born and raised in Queens, I'm a proud product of the New York City public school system. I graduated from UC Berkeley with a BA in Political Science and Interdisciplinary American Studies, and earned a certificate in Designing for Social Innovation and Leadership from the UN Centre for Peace.

I'm a highly organized and thoughtful professional, and aspire to merge my interests in technology, social innovation, and civic action in my career. I have experience working in tech, non-profit, and political consulting sectors. A former Board Member of the New York City chapter of the National Asian Pacific American Women’s Forum and Participatory Review Committee Member at the New York Women’s Foundation’s Fund for Girls and Young Women of Color, I'm also passionate about the intersections of gender and race. Most recently, I served as a Fulbright grantee in Malaysia where I taught English classes, managed peer mentorship and social entrepreneurship programs, facilitated skills-based technology workshops, and implemented public diplomacy initiatives.

In my free time, I enjoy traveling, hiking, and bicycling. I'm an adventurous hole-in-the-wall restaurant foodie. Someday, I hope to own a boba dance club.
