Sophia Ng
Class of 2014
Bachelor of Arts in Political Science, Bachelor of Arts in Interdisciplinary American Studies
My name is Sophia Ng. I respond to "Soph," so feel free to call me that too. I was born and raised in New York City. In August of 2011, I moved across the country to the San Francisco Bay Area for college. I also took a semester abroad at the University of Hong Kong where I studied journalism, globalization, and politics. In December of 2014, I earned two Bachelor of Arts degrees in Political Science and Interdisciplinary American Studies from the UC Berkeley. I'm interested in the intersections among human rights, social justice, and social entrepreneurship.

As an undergraduate, I served as the Managing Editor for hardboiled, an Asian Pacific American (APA) newsmagazine that seeks to serve as a vehicle for activism by critically discussing intersectional identities in the APA community. During my sophomore year, I served as a Director of the ASUC Vote Coalition, a community organization that registered over 8,500 voters for the 2012 national elections. (True story: Once, I unwittingly tried to register Steve Wozniak to vote at the Greek Theater.) I also worked as a Research Assistant at the Charles and Louise Travers Department of Political Science where I researched racial cues in news media during the 2008 and 2012 election cycles. Off-campus, I've interned at a political consulting firm in New York City, a national civil rights non-profit in Washington, D.C., and a microfinance organization in San Francisco.

I aspire to travel and work abroad in my 20s in the fields of international human rights law and/or international development. Right now, I live and work in the city that never sleeps-- I seriously took NYC's 24/7 public transportation for granted while I was away! In my free time, I enjoy reading zines, listening to Simon & Garfunkel, and trying to rock climb. I often take photos of my food and don't care what you think.

Shout out to the Web Design Decal for teaching me HTML, CSS, and JavaScript!
