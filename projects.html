Here are some of my projects that I'm proud of:
  • In 2017, I was lucky enough to travel to ten different countries. Check out my GoPro video compilation on Vimeo!
  • I delivered a speech about my experiences navigating cultural diversity both in Malaysia and in the United States at the Fulbright English Teaching Assistantship (ETA) Showcase in Putrajaya.
  • As a Fulbright ETA grantee, I earned some cool credibility with my students when I facilitated the translation of a popular Bahasa Melayu song into English, recorded an MP3 track, and filmed a music video. Posting it all on YouTube was the coolest, apparently.
  • I led the creation of Celebrating Our Sisters: Asian Pacific American Women of New York City: A NAPAWF*NYC storytelling project to celebrate APA sisters of New York City.
  • While at Kickstarter, I worked on a project to audit our chargebacks ecosystem. I used SQL and Looker (a business intelligence software and big data analytics platform) to surface and analyze the data. I taught myself R to create data visualizations for my presentation to the team.
  • Channels for Change: Sesame Street’s Ability to Implement Civil Rights Ideology in Children’s Programming: I wrote a 50-something page senior thesis exploring the origins of Sesame Street. Let me tell you, Season 1 of Sesame Street was DOPE.
